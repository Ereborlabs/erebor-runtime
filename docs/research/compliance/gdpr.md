# GDPR

## What It Is For

GDPR governs personal data processing for people in the EU/EEA. It emphasizes
lawful, fair, transparent, purpose-limited, minimized, secure, and accountable
processing.

## Evidence Companies Need

- records of processing activities
- lawful basis and consent/objection records
- DPIAs
- data protection by design/default evidence
- data-subject request workflows
- deletion, rectification, and export evidence
- processor contracts
- breach logs
- access controls
- transfer assessments

## What Erebor Can Do

Erebor can prove and enforce how AI agents act on personal data: which
user/agent accessed what, why, under which policy, through which browser, SaaS
app, command, API, or tool, and with what approval, when those actions are
routed through Erebor-controlled execution paths. This can support
purpose-bound execution, DSAR traceability, incident reconstruction, and
processor oversight.

## Artifacts Erebor Could Generate

- AI-agent RoPA supplement
- DPIA technical-safeguards annex
- personal-data access and export audit report
- DSAR traceability report for agent actions
- deletion/rectification workflow evidence
- breach investigation timeline

## Do Not Claim

Erebor does not determine lawful basis, controller/processor status,
data-subject-right completeness, breach reportability, transfer legality, or
retention requirements. It does not replace a DPO/privacy program, complete
DPIAs, or guarantee GDPR compliance. Erebor logs may themselves be personal data
and need retention/access controls.
