# NIST AI RMF

## What It Is For

NIST AI RMF is a voluntary framework for managing AI risks. Its functions are
Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage. It is one of the strongest matches for
Erebor because it cares about the boundary between an AI system's intended use,
its actual operating context, monitoring, and risk management.

## Evidence Companies Need

- AI roles and responsibilities
- intended use and deployment context
- affected users and systems
- laws, norms, and risk tolerance
- impact and risk assessments
- testing, evaluation, validation, and verification
- monitoring, incidents, recovery, and residual-risk decisions

## What Erebor Can Do

Erebor maps especially well to Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage for
computer-using and tool-using agents. It can prove who or what acted, what
policy applied, what action was allowed or denied, what systems/data/tools were
in scope, and whether the agent stayed inside the approved boundary when
actions are routed through Erebor-controlled execution paths.

This is one of the best first wedge stories because it is close to the product:
runtime evidence for agentic risk.

## Artifacts Erebor Could Generate

- NIST AI RMF evidence mapping matrix
- deployment-context and agent-boundary dossier
- runtime policy-conformance report
- approval, exception, and denial log
- incident replay and residual-risk packet
- third-party tool/SaaS access risk report

## Do Not Claim

NIST AI RMF is not a certification. Erebor should not claim to implement all AI
RMF outcomes or decide fairness, privacy, legal, societal-impact, residual-risk,
or risk-acceptance questions. It covers the runtime/action governance surface.
