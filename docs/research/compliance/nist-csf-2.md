# NIST CSF 2.0

## What It Is For

NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 helps organizations manage cybersecurity risk
through Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. It is
outcome-based and non-prescriptive, so Erebor fits as a mapped evidence source
for the AI-agent action surface rather than as a complete CSF program.

## Evidence Companies Need

- governance and risk-management records
- asset and identity inventories
- protective controls
- monitoring and detection evidence
- incident response and recovery evidence
- supplier and third-party risk records

## What Erebor Can Do

Erebor can map governed agentic activity into the CSF functions: Govern through
policies and owners, Identify through agent/tool/system inventory, Protect
through runtime blocking and approvals, Detect through action monitoring,
Respond through incident timelines, and Recover through rollback/remediation
evidence.

## Artifacts Erebor Could Generate

- AI-agent CSF profile
- governed action inventory
- high-risk action approval/denial report
- anomaly and policy-violation log
- incident reconstruction packet
- recovery/remediation transcript

## Do Not Claim

Erebor does not implement a whole cybersecurity program. It does not create an
official NIST profile, determine CSF tiers, own supplier risk management, or
cover activity outside Erebor-controlled execution paths. It provides runtime
control and evidence for the AI-agent action surface.
