# SOC 1

## What It Is For

SOC 1 reports cover controls at a service organization that are relevant to a
user entity's internal control over financial reporting.

## Evidence Companies Need

- service organization control description
- controls relevant to financial reporting
- Type 1 design/implementation evidence or Type 2 operating-effectiveness
  evidence, depending on report type
- access and change-management evidence
- complete, accurate, authorized, and timely processing evidence around
  financially relevant services
- complementary user entity controls and subservice organization treatment where
  applicable

## What Erebor Can Do

Erebor is relevant when AI agents can affect financially relevant systems or
processes: ERP workflows, reporting pipelines, database changes, SaaS admin
actions, API/MCP/tool actions, data exports, or scripts. It can produce a clear
runtime trail for what the agent did and who approved it when those actions are
routed through Erebor-controlled execution paths.

## Artifacts Erebor Could Generate

- agent action report for financially relevant workflows
- change-management evidence pack
- access and privileged-action review
- exception/override ledger
- population/sample export for management or auditor testing
- incident or suspected-misstatement agent-action timeline

## Do Not Claim

Erebor does not replace a SOC 1 report, CPA judgment, auditor testing,
management assertions, control objectives, materiality decisions, user-auditor
reliance decisions, subservice organization treatment, or ICFR design. It can
support evidence for agent-affected controls routed through Erebor-controlled
execution paths.
